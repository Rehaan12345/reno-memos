SUBJECT:  December 2025 City Walk Outcomes 
The City Walks Program is designed to enhance quality of life for residents while accelerating 
service delivery to vulnerable populations. These walks are data-driven and strategically aligned 
with broader organizational initiatives.  
Coordinated by Clean and Safe, through this effort staff proactively addresses emerging Reno 
Direct service request trends, partners with community members to address concerns, and 
coordinates with the Reno Police Department’s DEEDS (Directed Engagement and Enforcement 
Deployment Strategies) zones as a mechanism to connect residents, services, and enforcement 
efforts. Via this integrated and interdepartmental approach, City Walks promote proactive 
problem-solving and timely interventions in the areas of need.  
Walks occur four days per week. Monday and Wednesday walks are coordinated based on 
service request data citywide, while Tuesday and Thursday walks focus on the Business 
Improvement District (BID) in coordination with the Downtown Reno Partnership. 
City Walks Departments 
• Licensing and Code Enforcement  
• Community Engagement and Services – Clean and Safe 
• Parks and Recreation – Park and River Rangers, based on location 
• Reno Police – Community Action & Outreach (CAO), Mobile Engagement Team (MET), 
and Parking Enforcement

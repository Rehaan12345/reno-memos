SUBJECT: Actions to Address Fire Risk in Landscape Maintenance District (LMD) Areas 
In response to recent articles and inquiries regarding fire risk associated with vegetation 
conditions in Landscape Maintenance District (LMD) areas, the Reno Fire Department (RFD) 
coordinated with the Parks and Recreation Department to review current maintenance practices 
and identify actions to reduce wildfire risk adjacent to residential areas. 
Background 
Landscape Maintenance Districts (LMDs) are maintained by the Parks Maintenance Division 
through a contracted landscaping company, with oversight provided by City staff. Routine 
maintenance includes vegetation management, weed removal, debris cleanup, and clearing along 
fence lines and other landscaped areas. 
Wildfire defensible space guidelines recommend maintaining approximately five feet of 
clearance from homes, outbuildings, and fences to reduce the potential for fire spread. In areas 
where larger trees cannot reasonably be removed, pruning is recommended to prevent limbs from 
overhanging fences or directing fire toward adjacent properties. 
Findings and Actions 
• Vegetation Maintenance Oversight – LMD areas are maintained by a contracted 
landscaping company operating under the direction and supervision of Parks 
Maintenance staff. Maintenance activities include weed removal, debris cleanup, and 
vegetation clearing along fence lines and landscaped areas. Identified issues or 
deficiencies are addressed as soon as possible. 

 
 
 
• Defensible Space Implementation –A five-foot vegetation clearance along fence lines 
will be established within LMD areas in accordance with defensible space guidelines. 
Implementation will prioritize the removal of combustible vegetation while recognizing 
that certain existing, maintained vegetation may be retained where removal is not 
practical or would conflict with established maintenance objectives. In cases where large 
diameter trees are to remain, pruning will be conducted to reduce overhanging limbs and 
minimize potential fire pathways. Application of these standards may vary based on site 
conditions and the relative severity of wildfire risk in specific areas. 
 
• Contract Review and Updates – The current LMD maintenance contract expires 
September 30 and will be rebid this summer. RFD will assist Parks and Recreation in 
reviewing contract specifications to ensure vegetation maintenance requirements align 
with defensible space and wildfire risk reduction practices. 
 
• Training and Coordination – RFD will provide defensible space training and wildfire 
risk reduction guidance to the contracted landscaping company to support consistent 
maintenance practices. 
 
• Site Verification – RFD will work with Parks to conduct site visits in LMD areas to 
verify that defensible space guidelines and vegetation management practices are being 
implemented appropriately. 
These coordinated efforts between RFD and Parks and Recreation are intended to strengthen 
vegetation management practices, reduce wildfire risk near residential areas, and ensure 
defensible space principles are consistently applied within City-maintained landscape areas.

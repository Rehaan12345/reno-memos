SUBJECT:  January 2026 City Walk Outcomes 
The City Walks Program is designed to enhance quality of life for residents while expediting 
service delivery to vulnerable populations.  
Coordinated by Clean and Safe, City Walks are data-driven and strategically aligned with 
broader organizational initiatives. Using an interdepartmental approach, walks promote proactive 
problem-solving, timely interventions to address emerging Reno Direct service request trends, 
partners with community members to address concerns, and coordinates with the Reno Police 
Department’s DEEDS (Directed Engagement and Enforcement Deployment Strategies) zones as 
a mechanism to connect with residents, businesses, services, and enforcement efforts. 
Walks take place four days per week, Monday to Thursday, including two days dedicated to 
walks within the Business Improvement District (BID) in partnership with the Downtown Reno 
Partnership. 
City Walks Departments 
• Community Engagement and Services – Clean and Safe
• Licensing and Code Enforcement
• Parks and Recreation – Park and River Rangers, based on location
• Reno Police – Community Action & Outreach (CAO), Mobile Engagement Team (MET),
and Parking Enforcement

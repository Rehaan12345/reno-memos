SUBJECT: Parking Program Updates – February 2026 
Revenue Updates: 
On June 20, 2025, the City of Reno reinstated access to the Nevada Department of Motor 
Vehicle’s registered owner data after nearly 10 months, enabling staff to obtain addresses for 
unpaid parking citations. 
Parking Revenue and tickets paid to date are as follows: 
• June 20, 2025-December 31, 2025: 15,655 parking tickets paid totaling $530,534.89 in
fines and fees.
• January 1-January31, 2026: 1,295 parking tickets paid totaling $48,746.21 in fines and
fees.
• February 1-February 28, 2026: 1,206 parking tickets paid totaling $54,058.75 in fines and
fees.
• The total amount collected since reinstating access to the registered owner data on
June 20, 2025 is: $633,339.85.
Staff will continue to monitor revenue collections on a weekly basis, and unpaid citations may be 
sent to collections if payments are not remanded. 
Adjudication: 
RMC 6.30.080 requires respondents to either pay the civil fine or request a hearing, where they 
must prove any defense. The Hearing Officer may dismiss the citation, reduce the fine, or set a 
payment plan. Decisions may be appealed to Reno Municipal Court within ten days.

Software Updates: 
The vendor is currently working on obtaining a quote to send mail notifications for unpaid 
parking citations to registered vehicle owners in California. Once the quote is received, staff will 
review the costs and next steps for implementing the mailing process for collection. 
There is no update at this time regarding Nevada DMV holds. The City’s vendor (AIMS) is still 
awaiting action from the Nevada Department of Public Safety at the state level to complete the 
setup request. 
Staff continues to work with AIMS to develop new reports with additional metrics. Once the 
expanded reports are available, they will be incorporated into future memos. 
IPS Meter Status: 
IPS (the City’s current single space meter vendor) has been providing technician support to assist 
with replacing solar panels and batteries for the single‑space meters. Technician support began 
the week of January 5, 2026, and remains ongoing. Inclement weather impacted technician travel 
and field work. Staff now anticipates that all battery‑critical meter issues will be resolved by the 
end of March.

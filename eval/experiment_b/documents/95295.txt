SUBJECT: Love Your Block Program Year Two Updates 
This memo provides an overview of the second year of the Love Your Block grant program 
within the Housing and Neighborhood Development (HAND) Department.   
Due to the success of Year 1, the City received Year 2 funding from Bloomberg’s Center for 
Public Innovation, enabling the planning of additional community events to further support 
project implementation and resident engagement. For the 2026 program year, Ward 1 was 
selected as the area of focus, in coordination with Code Enforcement based on a high 
concentration of residential code violations, including home-related concerns and debris.   
In 2026, the Love Your Block program will include two application and funding rounds, each 
serving a distinct purpose. The first grant cycle, referred to as the CommUNITY Grant, will open 
on February 13, 2026 and close on March 31, 2026. These grants will support community-driven 
projects within Ward 1 that beautify shared spaces, promote neighborhood cleanups, and bring 
residents together, continuing the momentum and spirit of last year’s initiatives while addressing 
key issues in the area.   
Following the application period, a subcommittee of staff members will review and score all 
submissions in April, with grant awards announced on April 22, 2026. Selected projects will 
move into the implementation phase from late April through the end of September, allowing 
sufficient time for coordination, execution, and completion.  
The second application round will open on May 1, 2026, and will accept applications on a rolling 
basis until available funding is exhausted. This funding round will focus on improvements to the 

exterior of individual homes within Ward 1, including yard enhancements, fence painting, 
planting, and similar projects.  
Based on rubric criteria that evaluate project scale and impact, the establishment of two distinct 
grant categories allows the program to support both larger-scale neighborhood initiatives and 
individual home improvements.

SUBJECT: Washoe County School District Internship Program 
To advance youth civic engagement and strengthen departmental capacity, the Community 
Engagement and Services Department launched a pilot high school internship program in fall 
2025 in partnership with the Washoe County School District’s (WCSD) Career Technical 
Education (CTE) program. Building on its success, positive feedback, and departmental interest 
generated, the program continued into spring 2026 semester. 
This program provides students with hands-on experience in local government while earning 
academic credit. While there is no cost to the City, there is an administrative investment related 
to collaborating with WCSD, supporting the application process, onboarding, training, and intern 
supervision. Based on interest, students are assigned to host departments and supervised by a 
designated staff mentor, ensuring structured oversight, accountability, and high-quality learning 
experience. 
Fall 2025 
 
During the Fall 2025 launch semester, four students were placed across four City departments, 
including: 
• City Clerk’s Office 
• Community Engagement and Services 
• Parks and Recreation 
• Public Safety Dispatch 

   
 
   
 
Interns contributed approximately 240 total internship hours and supported priority departmental 
functions including administrative support, constituent communications, supporting front desks, 
research, and event coordination. They also developed practical skills and gained insight into 
public service, contributing to a more engaged and informed community. Key outcomes included 
improved turnaround time for routine tasks, increased staff capacity during peak periods, and 
exposure for students to public-sector careers. Feedback from both student interns and City staff 
was consistently positive, with student interns citing increased confidence and career clarity, and 
departments noting the value of added support and fresh perspectives. 
Spring 2026 
The City welcomed eight students this semester. They are currently serving six departments and 
divisions and will provide a total of 480 internship hours this semester. Participating departments 
include: 
• Arts and Culture 
• City Clerk’s Office 
• Clean and Safe  
• Licensing and Code Enforcement 
• Parks and Recreation 
At the conclusion of the current academic year, staff will evaluate the program, including 
available staff capacity, to determine long-term sustainability and possible program continuity.

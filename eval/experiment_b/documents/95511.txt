Revenue Updates:

Vehicle’s registered owner data after nearly 10 months, enabling staff to obtain addresses for 
unpaid parking citations.

Adjudication: 
Council has requested information regarding parking ticket appeals (adjudication). RMC 
6.30.080 requires a respondent to either admit an infraction by paying the civil fine or deny 
liability and request a hearing, with the burden on the respondent to prove any defense. At 

 
 
hearing, the Hearing Officer has discretion to dismiss the citation, reduce the assessed fine, or 
enter into payment arrangements in the interest of justice based on defenses such as vehicle theft, 
malfunctioning equipment, or emergency conditions. If liability is found and the fine is paid, the 
respondent may appeal the decision to Reno Municipal Court within ten days, where the matter 
proceeds as a civil action.

Software Updates: 
For the first time, the City of Reno now has access to registered vehicle owner information from 
the California DMV . This will allow staff to obtain addresses for individuals with unpaid parking 
citations whose vehicles are registered in California. 
The final setup work for Nevada DMV holds is moving forward. Currently, AIMS (software 
vendor) is waiting for the Department of Public Safety at the state level to process the request. 
Staff is also working with AIMS to develop new reports with additional metrics. Once the 
expanded reports are available, they will be incorporated in future memos. 
IPS Meter Status: 
At the November 19, 2025 City Council meeting, Council approved an agreement with IPS 
Group (the City’s current single space meter vendor) for gateway merchant fees, and parking 
meter maintenance parts for a one-year term, with the option to renew up to four additional years 
in an amount not to exceed $200,000. IPS also agreed to send technician support to assist with 
replacing solar panels and batteries for the single space meters. Technician support began the 
week of January 5, 2026, and is ongoing. Staff anticipates that all battery-critical meter issues 
will be resolved by the end of February.

SUBJECT: Washoe County Emergency Management Evacuation Study and Zoning   
Washoe County Emergency Management (WCEM) is leading a comprehensive regional 
evacuation study to strengthen community resilience in the face of wildfires and other 
emergencies that may require evacuation. This work is being conducted in close collaboration 
with local agencies, regional partners, and the community. It is designed to improve safety, 
coordination, and clarity during emergency events. 
Evacuations during wildfire or emergency events follow a coordinated, multi-agency process. 
Fire agencies evaluate conditions and determine when an area is unsafe, law enforcement 
agencies implement evacuations and ensure routes remain clear, and Emergency Management 
coordinates public communication, alerts, maps, and resource support. This study is intended to 
strengthen coordination across all phases of that process. 
To support this effort, WCEM has retained two professional vendors: Perimeter and CNA. 
Perimeter is providing traffic modeling to better understand evacuation movement and roadway 
capacity, while CNA is supporting data analysis and updates to the regional evacuation plan. The 
study is currently focused on the development of standardized evacuation zones that will be used 
by first responders during real-world incidents for orders such as “shelter-in-place” or 
“evacuate.” These zones will be used internally by first responders through the Perimeter 
platform and are intended to replace past practices where evacuation areas were manually drawn, 
sometimes resulting in unclear or inconsistent boundaries, including lines cutting through 
individual properties. The goal is to improve accuracy, consistency, and operational effectiveness 
during emergencies. 

 
 
WCEM and regional partners recognize the importance of local knowledge in this process. 
Workshops are being planned with first responders and partner agencies, including Reno Fire 
Department, Reno Police Department, Reno Public Works, Parks, Perimeter, Washoe County 
GIS, and WCEM. These sessions will focus on building evacuation zones that reflect how 
neighborhoods function in real life, including access points, road networks, and community 
characteristics. 
Residents are being invited to participate in developing evacuation zones through surveys, 
interactive tools such as community mapping, and public meetings. These tools allow 
community members to share past evacuation experiences, identify neighborhood-specific 
challenges, and suggest practical improvements. Public input is being reviewed alongside 
technical data to inform decisions. Ongoing WCEM updates will explain how feedback is 
shaping the outcomes in order to build an evacuation plan which prioritizes safety, coordination, 
and clarity. Those interested in participating can access the Swift Exit: Evacuation Study, which 
closes on March 30th, 2026 at 4:59pm.

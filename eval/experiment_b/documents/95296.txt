SUBJECT: Approved Position Update 
This memo provides an update on current vacant positions and the City’s process for determining 
whether and when vacancies are filled. Positions are measured in "Full Time Equivalent" (FTE), 
where one FTE equates to one full-time position. For Fiscal Year 2026 (FY26), the City adopted 
1,528.25 FTE’s and Redevelopment Agency (RDA) 10.25 FTE’s for a combined FTE count of 
1,538.50.  
 
Approved positions are reviewed annually during the budget adoption process. The authorized 
positions by function can be found in the Fiscal Year 2026 Annual Budget Book – Personnel 
Changes. Any increase to the City's overall approved FTE count is brought forward to Council 
for review and approval quarterly through the budget augmentation process.  Vacancies occur 
regularly throughout the fiscal year and fluctuate over time, particularly as Public Safety 
departments align hiring with academy schedules. 
 
Throughout the fiscal year, as vacancies arise, departments evaluate their current staffing levels 
to determine the necessity of filling the vacancy. If a department identifies a position as critical, 
they must submit a request for review through the City Manager's Office (CMO). The review 
process involves several key steps. First, the CMO assesses the operational need, followed by 
Human Resources evaluating the appropriateness of the classification for the position, if changes 
are recommended to the classification, it is reviewed by Civil Service and presented to the Civil 
Service Commission for recommendation, and finally, the Finance Department calculates cost 
recovery timelines and verifies there is sufficient funding available. When representatives from 
all three departments agree on a recommendation, it is provided to the City Manager who makes 

 
 
the final determination on whether to proceed with the recruitment process. 
 
The City regularly experiences vacancies throughout the fiscal year. Vacancy numbers fluctuate, 
especially as Public Safety departments prepare for training academies. Table 1 summarizes 
current vacancies, as of December 15, 2025. It includes twenty (20) positions that were frozen 
and unfunded at FY26 budget adoption, eight (8) positions frozen with the reduction in force 
(RIF), twenty-six (26) actively in recruitment, forty-four (44) currently vacant, and twelve (12) 
Police and Fire positions awaiting the next academy.

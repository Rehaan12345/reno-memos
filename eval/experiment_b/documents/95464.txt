SUBJECT: Virginia Range Horse Fencing Progress Update 
This memo provides an update on the Virginia Range Horse Protection Project, including:

summary of expenditures and funding sources.  
• Phase 2 (South Phase): Planned extension south from Kenneth Way to Toll Road to 
connect into fencing already installed by the Nevada Department of Agriculture.

PHASE 2 – South Phase (Kenneth Way to Toll Road) 
Project Update:  
On December 11, 2024, Council authorized the City Manager to award a contract for the Horse 
Protection Project – South Phase in accordance with competitive bidding laws in an amount not 
to exceed $660,000 from General Capital Funds. The first set of bids were received on May 1, 
2025, and staff were ultimately unable to recommend the award of a bid due to issues with 
obtaining private property easements necessary for fence construction and the need to re-align 
the proposed fence. Staff worked with the consultant, J-U-B Engineers and local horse advocates 
– Wildhorse Connection, to finalize and obtain easements to be able to bid the project a second 
time.  
The second round of bids were opened on December 2, 2025, and both bidders were non-
responsive, submitting incomplete bid packages. Staff waited until after the new year to 
readvertise the bid package for a third time. Bids were opened on January 22, 2026, with two 
responsive bidders. The low bidder, Soil Tech, Inc., submitted a bid in the amount of $686,335, 
which exceeds the $660,000 that the Council authorized the City Manager to sign for.  
Staff will bring an item on the February 25, 2026, agenda recommending that council authorize 
the approval of the bid award ($686,335) + contingency ($16,019) for a total of $702,354. 
Funding is available as outlined in Table 3 below.  
Completion of the portion of fencing between Kenneth Way and a recently completed fence by 
the Nevada Department of Agriculture on Toll Road, is critical. Without this section of fence in 
place, horses will be funneled down Toll Road or Geiger Grade to South Reno where we have 
previously experienced vehicular and horse collisions. Please see the fencing alignment at the 
end of this memo.

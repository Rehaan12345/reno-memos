SUBJECT: Parking Program Updates – December 2025 
This memo shall serve to update Council on the Parking Program since the last memo to 
Council, dated December 18, 2025. 
Revenue Updates: 
On June 20, 2025, the City of Reno reinstated access to the Nevada Department of Motor 
Vehicle’s registered owner data after nearly 10 months, enabling staff to obtain addresses for 
unpaid parking citations. 
Parking Revenue and tickets paid to date are as follows:  
• June 20-June 30, 2025: 490 parking tickets paid totaling $15,611.39 in fines and fees.  
• July 1-July 31, 2025: 3,972 parking tickets paid totaling $120,735.94 in fines and fees.  
• August 1-August 31, 2025: 4,436 parking tickets paid totaling $128,398.64 in fines and 
fees.  
• September 1-September 30, 2025: 2,488 parking tickets paid totaling $100,946.34 in 
fines and fees.  
• October 1-October 31, 2025: 1,844 parking tickets paid totaling $65,130.45 in fines and 
fees.  
• November 1-November 30, 2025: 1,165 parking tickets paid totaling $43,736.32 in fines 
and fees. 
• December 1-December 31, 2025: 1,260 parking tickets paid totaling $55,975.81 in fines 
and fees.   
• The total amount collected since reinstating access to the registered owner data is: 
$530,534.89. 

 
 
Staff will continue to monitor revenue collections on a weekly basis, and unpaid citations may be 
sent to collections if payments are not remanded. 
Software Updates: 
Staff continued working closely with the contracted software vendor, AIMs, and have 
successfully transitioned from an on-site system (housed on our own servers) to a cloud-hosted 
system. This transition allows for the software to run on secure, professionally managed servers 
off-site. Staff expects this to improve reliability, reduce maintenance needs, and allow the vendor 
to apply updates and fixes more efficiently. 
Now that this transition is completed, the final setup work for DMV holds can move forward. 
Currently, AIMS is waiting for the Department of Public Safety at the state level to process the 
request. 
IPS Meter Status 
At the November 19, 2025 City Council meeting, Council approved an agreement with IPS 
Group (the City’s current single space meter vendor) for gateway merchant fees, and parking 
meter maintenance parts for a one-year term, with the option to renew up to four additional years 
in an amount not to exceed $200,000. IPS also agreed to send technician support to assist with 
replacing solar panels and batteries for the single space meters. IPS Group technician support 
began the week of January 5, 2026. Additional updates on the progress will be provided in next 
month’s memo.

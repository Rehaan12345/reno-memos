SUBJECT: 2026 Reno Police Department Beat-Based Town Halls and Neighborhood   
  Advisory Board Presentations       
In 2026, the Reno Police Department (RPD) will expand its community engagement efforts 
through a series of police beat-based town halls and annual presentations to Neighborhood 
Advisory Boards (NABs). These structured forums will provide residents, City leadership, and 
RPD staff with an open and transparent setting to share timely public safety information, hear 
localized concerns, and partner on practical, community-driven solutions. 
Each town hall will emphasize: 
• Beat-specific crime trends and statistics 
• Beat-specific public safety concerns 
• Community policing initiatives 
• Mental health and policing 
Five town halls are planned and will be organized by police beat to ensure comprehensive 
geographic coverage: two in the North Beat (Wards 1, 4, and 5), one in the Central Beat (Wards 
1, 2, and 3), and two in the South Beat (Wards 2, 3, and 6). Each town hall will feature a 
moderated presentation followed by a facilitated question-and-answer session. The inaugural 
town hall will be held on February 12th, 2026 at North Valleys High School, which can 
accommodate up to 500 attendees and supports live streaming by City staff. Doors will open at 
5:00 p.m., and the program will begin at 6:00 p.m. 

 
 
To support consistent engagement and accessibility, informational resource booths will be 
available before and after the program. The City of Reno’s Rolling Recreation Center will also 
be onsite, offering yard games, sports balls, table games, and arts and crafts at no cost to 
attendees, and food trucks will be stationed in the parking area with items available for purchase. 
This model will be replicated for future town halls to ensure consistent services and outreach. A 
detailed agenda is being finalized to support effective moderation, time management, and 
discussion of community priorities. In advance of the first town hall, community questions and 
feedback will be collected via askrpd@reno.gov to help prioritize topics and ensure each session 
is responsive to the most pressing beat-specific concerns.   
The Communications Department is coordinating promotion of the town halls as details are 
finalized. A press release is in development, and a media availability with Chief Nance and 
Mayor Schieve is scheduled on January 29, from 1:00 to 1:30 p.m., at the Public Safety Center to 
launch this year's events. Outreach will also include newsletters, social media, and broadcast 
placements in advance of each town hall, following a consistent promotional cadence. 
Future anticipated 2026 dates are as follows (beat assignments confirmed). Specific venues and 
dates will be published once finalized:   
• Late April 2026 (South Beat) 
• Late June 2026 (Central Beat) 
• Early August 2026 (North Beat) 
• Late October 2026 (South Beat) 
Finalizing dates and locations remains a priority to ensure timely public notice and adequate 
opportunity for community participation. 
In addition to the town halls, RPD will conduct annual presentations at all Neighborhood 
Advisory Boards (NABs). These presentations will focus on ward-specific public safety 
concerns, crime statistics, and RPD initiatives supporting community policing. 
Council will receive invitations to all town halls and advance notice of their scheduled NAB 
presentation dates once the calendar is finalized. For each town hall, Council will have a 
designated greeting area to engage with attendees before and after the program. Additional 
information will be provided as details are confirmed.
